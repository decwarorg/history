In the context of Artificial Intelligence, "
In Defense of Logic
" refers to a seminal 1977 paper by Patrick J. Hayes that addressed a growing rift in the field during the late 1960s and 70s. At the time, AI researchers were divided between two competing philosophies: 
Logicists ("Neats"): Led by figures like John McCarthy, this group believed AI should be built on formal mathematical logic and the manipulation of explicit facts.
Proceduralists ("Scruffies"): This group argued that intelligence is too messy for rigid logic and instead favored "procedural" knowledge—essentially instructions or code—embedded in systems like PLANNER or KRL
